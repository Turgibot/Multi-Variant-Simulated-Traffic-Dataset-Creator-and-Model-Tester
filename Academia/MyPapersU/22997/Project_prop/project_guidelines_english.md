# Guidelines for Preparation, Writing and Submission of Project Proposals

## Purpose of the Proposal
The purpose of the proposal is to formulate in an organized manner a topic and direction for the project that meets academic standards. The proposal should be written in a way that demonstrates familiarity with the subject, knowledge of possible information sources, and the formulation of a worthy work plan that will lead to writing a project at an appropriate level and within a reasonable time.

We recommend that the project be an implementation of the topic of the final work or thesis. After approval of the project proposal, work on it can begin.

## Topic and Supervisor Selection
The stage of selecting a supervisor and topic is one of the important stages in the process and it is recommended to invest time and thought in it. The project coordinator will be able to help you at this stage. The process of selecting a topic for the project involves reading material related to the topic. It is recommended to contact potential supervisors and seek their help in formulating and defining the topic. A list of supervisors and possible topics that each of them offers can be found on the Computer Science homepage (http://telem.openu.ac.il/cs - Master's Degree, Advanced Project in Computer Science). You can also find examples of project proposals submitted in the past on this site.

## Preparing the Proposal
After selecting the topic and supervisor, a detailed proposal of objectives and work methods should be submitted, up to 3 pages in scope. At this stage, relevant material for the project should be collected and a general direction should be formulated. The preparation of the proposal is done under the guidance and approval of the supervisor.

It is important that the proposal be submitted in an appropriate manner both from the content aspect and from the formal aspect (see Appendix A). Care should also be taken for proper linguistic editing and style. Also, care should be taken for concise and accurate formulation and logical connection between paragraphs.

The proposal should include date, personal details and project details as detailed below:

### Personal Details:
- Student name; Student ID; Address; Phone; Email

### Project Details:
- **Project name**
- **Supervisor name**
- **Project objective** (a few lines)
- **Background with reference to sources** (about one page)
- **Project description** (a few lines)
- **Project importance** (a few lines)
- **Methods for project execution** (what types of activities will be performed - reading, analysis, programming, etc. Tools should be detailed - software, etc. that will be used)
- **Project execution timeline** (detailed at monthly level)
- **Initial source list** (the proposal body should reference sources. Sources should be written according to accepted rules in professional literature, as detailed in Appendix B)

## Proposal Submission
The project proposal should be submitted for supervisor approval, along with the accompanying project proposal form, which appears on the Master's website. The supervisor will forward the approved proposal along with the accompanying form to the Master's Committee.

## Proposal Review and Approval
The Master's Committee will review the project proposal and consult with additional readers for opinions as needed. The committee may decide in coordination with the supervisor one of these decisions:

a. **Approve the proposal**
b. **Require the student to make corrections approved by the supervisor only**
c. **Require the student to make corrections and then forward the proposal to additional readers for further reading**
d. **Reject the proposal**

The committee's decision will be communicated directly to the student by the Master's Committee, with a copy to the supervisor.

## Appendix A: Proposal Editing

The proposal should be printed, edited and submitted according to academic writing rules and according to Open University guidelines as follows:

### Font and Size:
- **Font:** David font, size 12
- **Notes in smaller font**

### Line Spacing:
- **In the project body:** One and a half lines
- **In sources and footnotes:** Single spacing

### Margins:
- **Right margins:** 2-3 cm
- **Page size:** A4
